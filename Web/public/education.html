CPU

What is it?

The CPU, or the central processing unit, is the brain of a computer, containing all the circuitry needed to process input, store data, and output results.

The CPU is constantly following instructions of computer programs that tell it which data to process and how to process it.

learn more

GPU

What is it?

The GPU, or the graphics processing unit, is used in a wide range of applications, including graphics and video rendering.

GPUs dramatically accelerate additional workloads in high performance computing and other forms of heavy usage.

learn more

RAM

What is it?

The RAM, or the random access memory, gives applications a place to store and access data on a short-term basis. It stores the information your computer is actively using so that it can be accessed quickly.

The speed and performance of your system directly correlate to the amount of RAM you have installed.

learn more

PSU

What is it?

The PSU, or the power supply unit, supplies power to your entire system, and is a critical part of any PC.

Different PSUs are designed to work with different voltages. Check how many volts your wall socket produces before purchasing a PSU.

learn more

Cooling

What is it?

The cooling system of a PC is extremely important to keep the internal temperature of the PC in check. The hardware of the PC may crash and turn off if the internal temperature gets too high.

There are two types of cooling -- liquid cooling and air cooling.

learn more

Storage

What is it?

The storage components of a PC enable the PC to store data for applications and files. However, it can do more than just that.

There are two types of storages -- SSDs, or solid state drives, and HDDs, or hard disk drives.

learn more

Motherboard

What is it?

It is the circuit board that connects all of your hardware to your processor, distributes electricity from your power supply, and defines the types of storage devices, memory modules, and graphics cards (among other expansion cards) that can connect to your PC.

learn more

Case

What is it?

The PC case is the housing for all the parts of your PC. It serves as the first line of physical defence for all you exposed PC parts.

learn more

Thermal Compound

What is it?

The thermal compound acts as material to eliminate air gaps from the interface area of the CPU to the heat sink in order to maximize heat transfer.

learn more
